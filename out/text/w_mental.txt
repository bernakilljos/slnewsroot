1. Executive Summary  
1.1. Mental health is a priority in Wandsworth Council’s Corporate Plan. This 
report introduces the final version of ‘Healthy Minds’ Public Mental Health 
Action Plan, informed by the 2022 Mental Health Needs Assessment1. The 
plan outlines Council-led programmes requiring long-term delivery to 
improve population mental health and prevent mental–ill health which aligns 
with the Council’s vision for a fair, compassionate, and sustainable borough. 
It was discussed at the Health Committee on 18 November 2025 as 
requested by Cllr. Dobres. The Executive Director of Finance comments that 
there are no financial implications arising from this report currently.

3. Details  
3.1. This report presents the Council’s ‘Healthy Minds’ Public Mental Health 
Action Plan, a prevention-focused initiative designed to improve the mental 
health and wellbeing of Wandsworth residents. Rooted in the Council’s 
Prevention Framework, the plan promotes collaborative working across 
council departments, NHS partners, and the voluntary and community 
sector. It focuses on the social and environmental factors that shape mental 
health, recognising the Council’s significant influence through leadership on 
the building blocks of health. Organised around four thematic areas: Growth 
and Success, Healthy Living, Community and Environment, and Foundations 
for wellbeing, the plan highlights the pivotal role of Council-led policies in 
areas such as housing, education, public health, welfare, leisure, and social 
care in improving population mental health. 
 
3.2. The Public Mental Health Action Plan provides a strategic framework for a 
coordinated cross-council approach to improving population mental health. 
This ambitious initiative is fully aligned with the values of the Better Services 
Partnership, which include thinking bigger, putting people first, fostering 
connections, leading by example, and embracing difference. The plan 
introduces a matrix model that connects a diverse range of programmes 
addressing the building blocks of health. This is informed by local and 
national level insights from the 2022 Mental Health Needs Assessment1.  It 
highlights the essential preventative role of local authorities and clearly 
defines the Council’s contribution within the broader context of Integrated 
Care Partnerships. In addition, it complements the mental health strategies 
of the Integrated Care Board and the local mental health trust, thereby 
reinforcing a whole-system approach to mental health and wellbeing. 
 
3.3. The Public Mental Health Action Plan was well received at the Health 
Overview and Scrutiny Committee on 18 November 2025. Minor 
amendments were made to support Councillors’ suggestions and reflective 
of the final public consultation that closed on 18 December 2025. Clarifying 
language and additional diagram labels were added following consultation 
with the Youth Council.

6. Equalities Implications  
6.1. The Equality Act 2010 requires that the Council when exercising its functions 
must have "due regard" to the need to eliminate discrimination, to advance 
equality of opportunity between persons who share a relevant protected 
characteristic and persons who do not share it and to foster good relations 
between persons who share a relevant protected characteristic and persons 
who do not share it. As such an Equality Impact Needs Analysis (EINA) has 
been undertaken on the Public Mental Health Action Plan.  
 
6.2. This EINA has found increased prevalence, complexity, acuity, and longevity 
of mental health conditions. This is especially true considering the pandemic, 
the cost-of-living crisis, and accelerating climate change, all compounded by 
an increasingly volatile geopolitical landscape and limited resources for 
public services, which have further eroded the resilience of individuals and 
the systems that support them. Rising demands on mental health services 
are leading to increased wait times and insufficient care. The EINA also 
identified mental health disorders are more common in people living alone, in 
poor physical health or with a disability or who are unemployed. Service 
users with neurodiversity and/or learning disabilities, those with co-occurring 
mental health and substance misuse disorders, carers and Global Majority 
groups are particularly vulnerable. 
 
7. Supporting the Wandsworth Environment and Sustainability Strategy 
(WESS) 
7.1. The Public Mental Health Action Plan aligns with the ambitions of the 
Wandsworth Environment and Sustainability Strategy, identifying 
environmental improvement programmes as a key priority. By fostering a 
cleaner, greener, healthier, and safer borough, these efforts aim to enhance 
the mental health and wellbeing of residents.

8. Conclusion 
8.1. The Public Mental Health Action Plan sets out a strategic, prevention-focused 
framework for improving population mental health in Wandsworth. Grounded 
in the Council’s Prevention Framework and shaped through cross-directorate 
collaboration, the plan highlights the Council’s leadership role in addressing 
the building blocks of mental health. Cabinet is recommended to approve The 
Public Mental Health Action Plan to guide and champion the Council’s ongoing 
approach to improving mental health in Wandsworth.
