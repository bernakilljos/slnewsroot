1. Executive Summary  
A Task and Finish Group was established in response to the C3 grading by 
the Regulator of Social Housing following their inspection in late 2024 and 
the publication of their findings in February 2025. 
 
This report provides the details of the Social Housing Improvement Task and 
Finish Group’s work, noting the terms of reference and scope of the review, a 
summary of the Group’s meetings, the key matters that have been 
considered, and the findings, comments and recommendations of the Group. 
 
The report and its recommendations are due to be considered by the 
Housing Overview and Scrutiny Committee at their meeting on 21st January 
2026 and, subject to their approval of the recommendations, are submitted to 
Cabinet for consideration. 
 
 
2. Recommendation/s:  
2.1. Subject to approval at the Housing Overview and Scrutiny Committee, 
Cabinet is asked to receive the Task and Finish Group’s report (Appendix 1) 
and consider the following recommendations: 
 

 
 
Official
(1)  There be consideration of additional resource being established to assist 
with analysing data gathered during stock condition surveys. It is 
considered that having a clear picture and analysis of the stock condition 
data will enable senior leaders and members to prioritise investment based 
on resident safety and impact. 
 
(2)  Improve the resident repair journey by reducing appointment windows and 
including a digital / online tracking element so that residents can track the 
raising of their repair order, view their appointment slot, track lateness and 
reschedule their appointment online if necessary as well as provide 
feedback on the service immediately after. 
 
(3)  Co-design the repairs and maintenance contract specifications with residents 
and communicate with tenants how tenants’ voice has influenced service 
design. 
 
(4) Use resident provided feedback and data in robust monitoring of repairs and 
maintenance contracts. 
 
(5) The Borough Residents’ Forum constitution and future meeting dates 
should therefore be revised and updated to reflect the change in the 
Council’s governance. 
 
(6) Tenant and leaseholder representation be included in the governance 
arrangements monitoring performance and delivery of improvements to the 
Council’s managed housing stock. 
 
(7) Commission specialist external consultants on the engagement strategy, to 
include both the formal engagement structures including Residents 
Associations, and informal engagement processes, with a view to 
concluding their review by June 2026.
