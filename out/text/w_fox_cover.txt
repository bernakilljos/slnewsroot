1. Executive Summary  
1.1. On 10 September 2025, a major fire broke out at Fox House, a five-storey 
block of 37 flats in Battersea, displacing over 100 residents. The fire spread 
rapidly across the roof void, requiring 15 fire engines and 100 firefighters to 
bring it under control. No residents were injured, but significant property 
damage occurred, and people were left without homes. Bronze, Silver and 
Gold roles were activated overnight to respond. A Gold meeting was 
convened at 9:30am on 11th September and a Humanitarian Assistance 
Lead Officer (HALO) was appointed to lead on the psychosocial support 
response for residents, and a Community Assistance Centre was opened. 
 
1.2. On 8th October the Community Assistance Centre closed, the Gold group 
stood down, and the response transitioned into recovery. Households were 
appointed a named worker to support them through the transition into 
permanent accommodation. A recovery co-ordination group is sitting with 
representation from Housing, Finance and Insurance, Communications, and 
Emergency Planning. This is chaired by the Executive Director for Change 
and Innovation and has been monitoring the transition for residents into 
permanent accommodation, the progress on site, finance tracking and 
insurance claims, communications and capturing learning. 
 
1.3. The following is an independent report from Barry Quirk CBE PhD BSc FRGS, 
detailing the events of the night and reviewing Wandsworth Council’s 
response to the fire. The review took place throughout November and 
December 2025, and consulted residents, emergency services, Councillors

1.4. Barry Quirk is an experienced former Chief Executive and qualified in 
housing management. In June 2017, he was drafted by Government and the 
LGA to support the response to the Grenfell Tower fire tragedy. He was 
appointed in September 2017 as Chief Executive of the Royal Borough of 
Kensington and Chelsea (RBKC) and worked to reshape the Council and the 
relationship with the thousands of bereaved, survivors and local communities 
that were impacted by the Grenfell tower fire. 
 
1.5. As of publication of this report, all tenants of Fox House will have moved into 
new accommodation. Displaced residents impacted who were initially placed 
in a Travelodge, had all moved into their new accommodation prior to 
Christmas. The final resident moved into their new flat on 15th January 2026. 
 
2. Recommendation/s:  
2.1. Cabinet are requested to note the report and its recommendations and agree 
the actions set out below. 
 
3. Details 
3.1. In summary, Barry Quirk’s Independent Review commends Wandsworth 
Council for its strong response to the Fox House fire, highlighting examples 
of good practice such as the effective use of data. However, the report also 
identifies areas for improvement, particularly in the initial hours of the 
response. 
 
3.2. His recommendations are set out below alongside the Council’s actions to 
address them.
