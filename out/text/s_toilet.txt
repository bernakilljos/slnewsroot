Briefing: Public toilets - Action Plan Update 
Health and Social Care Scrutiny Commission 
27th January 2026 
Background 
This report provides an update on the work the council is doing to improve public toilet provision 
in Southwark. In October 2024 a cabinet report was presented in response to the review of 
public toilet provision in Southwark carried out by the Health and Social Care Scrutiny 
Commission.   Nine recommendations were made aimed addressing gaps in toilet provision, 
especially for vulnerable groups. The recommendations impact multiple council departments and 
require a coordinated response. The paper below sets out the ongoing cross departmental work.  
Update 
The management and development of council managed public toilet facilities across the borough 
is carried out by several different council departments. Officers from 10 different teams have 
been brought together to form a public toilet working group. The group meet once a quarter and 
membership also includes a resident of southwark who is involved in the campaign for improved 
public toilet provision.  
The aims of the group are to; 
 Deliver and develop the actions in the Toilet Action Plan (see appendix 1) 
 Improve the standards of provision of the council’s existing public toilets (See appendix 2) 
 Work with businesses and the wider community to improve the number of public toilets 
available 
 Improve communications to inform residents and visitors where they can find a public 
toilet  
 Where possible/feasible provide additional public toilets particularly in areas of low 
provision

Investment  
In addition to the ongoing delivery of the Toilet Action plan, officers across a number of teams 
have been working on various investment projects to either improve existing council public 
toilets or deliver new ones. Below are the projects that have either been delivered or are in the 
process of being delivered.  
Existing - Peckham Rye Park - Peckham Rye Park’s existing public toilet block is to be 
refurbished this spring, delivering a safe, inclusive and durable amenity for park visitors. 
Estimated to open in April 2026. 
The current facilities have not been refurbished for many years and have been subject to 
numerous arson attacks and antisocial behaviour incidents. The equipment and finishes are now 
damaged beyond repair and deep clean. The layout is restrictive and unwelcoming. 
The objective of the refurbishment is to provide good quarterly facilities that require low 
maintenance, have strong anti-vandal fixtures and facilities. Once the project has been 
completed the toilets will reopen from 7.30-21.00 in the summer and to dusk in the winter. They 
will be subject to a daily cleaning regime operated through the Corporate Facilities Maintenance 
Contract. 
Existing - East Street Market toilets – Officers are currently exploring a refurbishment project to 
improve the public toilets in the busy East Street market. The facilities are dated and subject to 
incidents of anti social behaviour. Plans and costs have been developed and officers are 
currently exploring opportunities for funding.  
Existing – Dulwich Library – The refurbishment of Dulwich library is currently underway and due 
for completion in July 2026. Part of the project is the refurbishment of the ground floor public 
toilets (including 1 accessible toilet); however, there will also be an additional toilet installed on 
the first floor.  
New provision - Brandon Library – As part of the refurbishment of the Brandon Estate Library is 
the provision of a new accessible public toilet. Works on the library are due to begin in February 
and will be completed in the summer.   
New provision - Canada Water Leisure Centre - The council’s new leisure centre opened in 
autumn last year. The facility includes a fully accessible new public toilet in the reception area, 
before the barriers to enter the facility. In addition to this, the centre also provides a Changing 
Places toilet which, whilst located within the centre, is open for use by anyone needing to use 
the changing places toilet.  
Officers across the council continue to explore the potential for the provision of new public toilets 
where feasible and sustainable, particularly in relation to the development of new public building 
and facilities.  
 
 

 
 
 
4 
Challenges 
The OSC report also made the recommendation to revive the council’s Community Toilet 
Scheme. Unfortunately, the position on this remains the same, which is that while much work is 
underway, with toilet information consolidated on the Great British Toilet Map and engagement 
with businesses ongoing, there is currently no budget for a more formal scheme with 
businesses. Benchmarking of the scheme in other areas shows that, success requires a need 
for monitoring and financial incentives for business to cover costs. 
The report also recommended the development of a comprehensive strategy through 
stakeholder engagement and mapping and the response was to commit to delivering an 
Accessible Toilet Plan that will ensure fair access, up-to-date information, and business 
engagement. 
Despite the resource challenges, there has been progress on the council’s work to improving 
public toilet provision. This includes: 
1. A more coordinated and collaborative cross departmental approach to the council’s work 
on improving public toilet provision.  
2. Completion of a number of capital investment schemes to improve provision of public 
toilets - with more to follow.  
3. Consolidation of data on public toilets and updating of the information on the Toilet Map 
online.  
4. The imminent delivery of a new council campaign, signposting residents on how they can 
find out where there nearest public toilet is, should they need one.   
5. A refreshed focus on working with local business to encourage them to provide public 
access to their customer toilets.
